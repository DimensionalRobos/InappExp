PhD Research Project

This is a Department for Employment and Learning (DEL) funded PhD Studentship in collaboration with Treze Ltd (Carnbane Business Centre, Newry, Northern Ireland). Applications are invited for the following DEL-CAST studentship (Co-operative Awards in Science and Technology). The project available is in the Computer Science Research Institute in collaboration with Treze Ltd and is tenable in the School of Computing and Mathematics, Faculty of Computing and Engineering at the Jordanstown Campus of the University of Ulster.

Background

Treze Ltd was established to develop intelligent software systems that will help parents know if their child is being cyber bullied. Currently the company has an innovation project with the academic superiors at the University of Ulster, supported by the Innovation Voucher Programme of Invest Northern Ireland. The aim of the project is to provide a feasible roadmap to the current product, which helps the company develop the product that has the ability to make sense of posts through text analytics filtering/classifying cyber bullying and posts of interest.

The work required for the student is to undertake research with the academic supervisors to improve the companys current detection algorithms for bullying identification, and develop a proof of concept prototype to automatically analysis mood disorders and inappropriate intimacy by incorporating state of the art technologies of sentiment analysis and data analytics technologies. It is envisaged that the student will have extensive contact and support with the collaborating company during the PhD study and have a certain period of time, such as a three months per year, to work at the company with the industrial supervisors in order to understand the company business needs, collect product requirements and use cases, and develop an intelligent algorithm in addition to carrying out appropriate evaluation.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Sentiment Analysis (or Opinion Mining) is the task of finding the opinions of people about specific textual entities. The decision-making process of people is usually affected by the opinions formed by domain authorities and the proliferation of online discussions. When a customer wants to buy a product, for instance, the customer will typically start by searching for views and opinions expressed by other people on the quality, services and various offerings about the product, which have been gleaned online. However, a popular product could normally receive reviews in the hundreds that cause difficulty for users in obtaining the information they require. There is therefore a need to make sense of reviews automatically, indicating aspects and sentiment in a way which is easy to read and compare for people. Sentiment analysis is aimed at catering for such a goal, which is currently one of the hottest research areas in computer science.

The proposed project aims to develop a text analytics model for sentiment analysis on the basis of our previous and ongoing work. The specific project research will be composed of four parts as detailed below:

Use machine learning technology to build lexicons as prior knowledge for making sense of textual entities, which will be fed to topic models to perform sentiment analysis at both the sentence level and document level.
Use natural language processing techniques such as part-of-speech as prior knowledge for making sense of textual entities, which will be fed to an analysis model (such as topic models) to perform sentiment analysis.
Investigate aspect extraction for cyber bullying and inappropriate cyber behaviours, which will be incorporated into sentiment analysis process.
Development of an effective algorithm by combining heuristic domain knowledge and topic models together, which will be used to infer compound scenarios of cyber bullying.

The research results will be implemented as a proof of concept prototype and evaluated on the basis of selected social networks to address the challenges in detecting cyber bullying and inappropriate cyber behaviours.

The closing date for receipt of completed applications is 20th February 2015.

Interviews will be held in March 2015.

If you wish to discuss your proposal or receive advice on the research project please contact: Dr Yaxin Bi, tel: 02890366582, email: y.bi@ulster.ac.uk ; Professor Maurice Mulvenna tel: 02890368602, email: md.mulvenna@ulster.ac.uk .

For further information on the application process please visit our website: http://study.ulster.ac.uk/postgraduate/applying.php

Apply online www.ulster.ac.uk/applyonline


Funding Notes:

Applicants should hold ordinary UK residence to be eligible for both fees and maintenance. Non UK residents who hold ordinary EU residence may also apply but if successful will receive fees only. All applicants should hold a 1st or 2.1 honours degree in Computer Science or a related discipline. Successful candidates will enrol on a full-time research programme, of up to three years subject to satisfactory progress, leading to the award of the degree of Doctor of Philosophy.

The studentship will comprise tuition fees and a maintenance award (subject to UK residence status) of not less than 15,000 per annum.
